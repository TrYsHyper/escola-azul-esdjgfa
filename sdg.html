SDG's and Us

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, provides “a blueprint to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all by 2030”. At its heart are the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which are an urgent call for action by all countries – developed and developing – in a global partnership. These goals include the end of poverty, gender equality, protection of life below water and on earth, and many others. You can check them all on the SDG website!

Our project – The World We Want - focuses on showing people what is the ideal world for all living beings and future generations, basically, the world we want to live in and the one we are trying to build. We attempt to motivate everyone to help us on this mission to achieve sustainability, together with the respect for nature and for all living beings (humans included). So, the SDGs are extremely important for us, because they are a blueprint to achieve the sustainable development, which is too our main objective. We are currently studying the SDGs and their targets, checking the movements and actions that are being done to achieve them, and doing everything we can to be a part of the change. We know we can make the difference, and the SDGs are the path to follow. This is why they are so important to us, and everyone should be aware of them.
